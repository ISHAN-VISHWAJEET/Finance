Budgeting

Budgeting refers to the process of creating a plan to spend your money wisely. It helps you balance your income and expenses, save for future goals, and avoid unnecessary debt.

Budgeting in India: The Government of India prepares an annual budget that outlines estimated revenues and expenditures for the upcoming financial year. It plays a crucial role in maintaining financial discipline and economic growth.

  • The Union Budget is presented every year by the Finance Minister in Parliament, usually in February.
  • It includes two main parts — the Revenue Budget and the Capital Budget.
  • The Revenue Budget covers government income (like taxes) and expenses (like subsidies and salaries).
  • The Capital Budget deals with investments, loans, and infrastructure development.
  • Personal budgeting helps individuals manage spending, save, and invest effectively.

Monetary System

The monetary system of a country refers to the framework through which money is created, managed, and circulated in the economy.

Monetary System in India: India follows a managed paper currency system, also called a fiat money system — which means our currency, the Indian Rupee (₹), is not backed by gold or silver, but by the trust in the Government of India and the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).

  • The official currency is the Indian Rupee (₹), issued and controlled by the RBI. One Rupee = 100 Paise.
  • The RBI, established in 1935 under the RBI Act of 1934, manages money supply, prints currency, controls inflation, and maintains economic stability.
  • All notes except the ₹1 note and coins are issued by the RBI; ₹1 note and coins are issued by the Government of India.
  • The RBI regulates money flow through tools like repo rate, reverse repo rate, CRR, and SLR.
  • India's digital payment systems — UPI, NEFT, RTGS, and IMPS — ensure fast, secure transactions.

Insurance System

The insurance system provides financial protection against unexpected losses or risks. It works on the principle of risk pooling — where many people pay small premiums so that the few who face loss are compensated.

Insurance System in India: India has a well-regulated insurance market governed by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI). It ensures the stability, transparency, and security of insurance companies and protects policyholders’ interests.

  • Insurance provides a safety net for individuals and businesses during financial hardships.
  • There are two major types — Life Insurance and General Insurance.
  • Life Insurance covers death or maturity benefits; General Insurance covers health, motor, property, etc.
  • IRDAI, established in 1999, supervises and promotes fair practices in the insurance sector.
  • Popular public insurers include LIC, New India Assurance, and Oriental Insurance.

Stock Market

The stock market is a platform where investors buy and sell ownership shares of companies, also known as stocks. It helps businesses raise capital and allows investors to earn profits through share price appreciation and dividends.

Stock Market in India: India’s stock market is among the largest in the world, driven by exchanges like the BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange) and NSE (National Stock Exchange). These exchanges list thousands of companies and provide a transparent system for trading. Learn More

  • Major stock indices are SENSEX (BSE) and NIFTY 50 (NSE).
  • Investors earn through capital gains (price rise) and dividends.
  • Trading can be short-term (trading) or long-term (investing).
  • Market prices depend on demand, company performance, and investor sentiment.
  • Digital platforms like Zerodha, Groww, and Upstox make investing easier for everyone.
