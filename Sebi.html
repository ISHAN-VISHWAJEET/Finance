How to invest in the Stock Market — SEBI & Investor Safety

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the regulatory body that protects investors and keeps India’s securities markets fair and transparent. Below is a concise guide to SEBI’s role, and how it helps you invest safely.

Introduction

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) was first created in 1988 and became a statutory body under the SEBI Act, 1992. Headquartered in Mumbai, SEBI's mandate is to protect investor interests, develop the securities market, and enforce regulations that ensure transparency and fairness.

Vision

To create a secure, transparent, and efficient securities market that inspires investor confidence and supports economic growth.

Mission

To safeguard investor interests, facilitate market development, and enforce rules that uphold integrity and accountability.

What We Do — SEBI's Major Roles

Regulatory Functions

Supervises exchanges, depositories and market intermediaries (brokers, underwriters). Frames rules to prevent insider trading and market manipulation.

Developmental Functions

Promotes investor education, adoption of modern trading systems, and simplifies procedures to encourage investment.

Protective Functions

Investigates unfair trade practices, runs the SCORES grievance system, and enforces disclosure norms to maintain market integrity.

A short timeline

1988 — SEBI set up as a non-statutory body.
1992 — SEBI Act enacted; SEBI becomes a statutory regulator.
2000s — present — Major reforms: electronic trading, improved disclosure, investor education drives.

Practical investor-facing actions by SEBI

  • Enforces KYC & transparency for brokers and funds.
  • Runs SCORES for investor grievances — you can file complaints and track them online.
  • Publishes circulars and investor advisories (subscribe to stay informed).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is SCORES?
▸

SCORES is SEBI's online grievance redressal system where investors can file and track complaints against intermediaries or listed companies.

How does SEBI protect retail investors?
▸

Through regulation of intermediaries, enforcement actions, investor education campaigns, and transparent disclosure norms for listed entities.

Where can I learn more about rules?
▸

Visit the SEBI website for circulars, investor guides, and latest regulatory updates.

Start Smart

Before investing: set goals, check your risk appetite, diversify, and practice with paper trading.

More about SEBI's protective tools

SEBI actively investigates market abuse, issues penalties, and ensures companies make timely disclosures. If you face unfair practices, lodge a complaint on SCORES and keep your KYC and transaction proofs handy.
